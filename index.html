Responsible Speech Generative AI

Xuanjun Chen1, Haibin Wu2, Sung-Feng Huang3, Yuanchao Li4, Hung-yi Lee1
1National Taiwan University, 2Microsoft, 3NVIDIA, 4University of Edinburgh
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Abstract

The field of speech generative AI is evolving rapidly, unlocking powerful capabilities in synthetic speech, voice cloning, and sound effect generation. These advancements are transforming domains such as media production, accessibility, and human-computer interaction. However, they also raise pressing concerns about responsibility, including potential issues of bias, misuse, and the lack of transparency in how generative models operate.

This special session aims to bring together the speech research community to explore the responsible development and deployment of speech generative systems. We welcome submissions that tackle challenges in controllability (e.g., guiding model outputs by style, emotion, or speaker identity), fairness (e.g., mitigating demographic and linguistic bias), explainability (e.g., interpreting model behavior and justifying outputs), and deepfake detection or prevention (e.g., identifying synthetic speech or designing robust safeguards).

The session will highlight technical innovations, evaluation methodologies, and frameworks that contribute to safer, fairer, and more trustworthy generative systems. It also aims to foster broader discussions around standards, best practices, and accountability in real-world deployments of speech generative technologies.
